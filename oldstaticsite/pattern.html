Tips & Tricks

Getting Started Right

Yarn Choice: For beginners, choose a yarn that is smooth and light-colored. Dark yarns make it harder to see your stitches, which can be frustrating when learning new techniques.

Stitch Markers: Use a distinct stitch marker to highlight the beginning of the round. This simple step prevents the most common mistake of losing your place in the pattern.

Blocking: Always wash and block your swatch before starting. Adaptive wear needs to fit precisely, and yarn can grow or shrink significantly after the first wash.

Expert Tips

Modifications for Mobility

Thumb Placement: For advanced knitters, adjusting the thumb gusset placement based on specific hand mobility issues is crucial. Consider moving the gusset 1-2 stitches forward for palmar abduction issues.

Float Management: When using colorwork, ensure your floats are caught every 3 stitches. Long floats can catch on fingers or medical equipment inside the glove, creating a safety hazard.

Tubular Bind-offs: Experiment with tubular bind-offs for a finish that is both visually professional and extremely elastic, perfect for swollen joints or fitting over casts.
